IF I COULD TELL THE MOON

prasiddha

If I could tell the moon my secrets deep,

Would she keep them safe while mortals sleep?

Would she understand the weight I bear,

Or simply glow, indifferent to my prayer?

I'd whisper hopes that never saw the day,

And dreams that time has slowly worn away;

I'd share the love I couldn't speak aloud,

Hidden beneath my silent, fearful shroud.

The moon has seen a thousand years unfold,

She's witnessed stories new and stories old;

Perhaps she knows that pain and joy entwine,

That broken hearts eventually align.

If I could tell the moon, would she reply?

Or would she just drift gently through the sky?

Maybe silence is her wisest art—

A listening ear, a sympathetic heart.
