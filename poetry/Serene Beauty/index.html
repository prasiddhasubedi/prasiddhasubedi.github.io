Serene Beauty

prasiddha

I think that I shall never find

A wonder more serene in mind

Than morning light upon the hill,

Where silence lingers soft and still;

A hill that greets the rising day

With golden breath and gentle sway;

A hill that keeps the secrets told

By wandering winds and sunsets bold;

A hill that wears a cloak of green,

In summer's warmth, a quiet queen;

And when the winter skies turn gray,

She holds her peace beneath the day.

Such simple grace belongs to earth—

No craft of mine can shape its worth.

Poets may dream and words may thrill,

But only time can shape a hill.
