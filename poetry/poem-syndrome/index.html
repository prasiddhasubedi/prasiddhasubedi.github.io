Poem Syndrome

Prasiddha
January 23, 2026


The poem arrives

softly,

like a truth

that doesn’t want

to be named yet.


I rush it.

Ask it to mean something.

Ask it to justify

the space it takes

on the page.


That’s when it starts coughing

too many thoughts,

too much control,

not enough silence.


I mistake clarity

for depth,

structure

for honesty.


I forget that some things

are meant to remain

slightly unfinished.

The poem wants to be felt,

not understood.


It wants to sit

between two heartbeats

and disappear.

So I loosen my grip.

Let the lines wander.

Let the ending

arrive late

or not at all.


The syndrome fades

when I stop demanding

a poem

and allow

a moment.

And the moment,

finally unobserved,

becomes enough.
