THE SKY BENEATH MY FEET

prasiddha

I walked upon the sky today,

Beneath my feet, the clouds did sway;

The world inverted, upside down,

I wore the heavens as my crown.

The earth above looked strange and small,

I felt no fear that I might fall;

For gravity had lost its hold,

And I was free, unbridled, bold.

I danced on air, I touched the blue,

I saw the world from angles new;

Perspective shifts when up is down,

When sky becomes the solid ground.

Maybe we're all walking blind,

Too rigid in our earthly mind;

Maybe truth is upside down,

Maybe freedom wears no crown.
